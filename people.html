People of Fuji


Mount Fuji is mainly just a tourist destination, but the region around Mount Fuji is still home to a diverse population. The local economy is primarily driven by tourism, agriculture, and small-scale manufacturing. Many residents work in the hospitality industry, running inns, hotels, and restaurants that cater to the influx of tourists. Agriculture in the region includes rice farming, tea cultivation, and wasabi farming. The people here are known for their hospitality and their deep respect for nature, which is reflected in the conservation efforts to preserve the beauty of Mount Fuji. The predominant religion is Shintoism, with many local shrines and festivals dedicated to honoring the mountain.
